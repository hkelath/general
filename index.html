Your Family Is Not Alone

Chronic pain is a significant challenge facing many young people. Understanding its prevalence is the first step in recognizing the scale of the issue and de-stigmatizing your daughter's experience.

A Widespread Challenge

The Vicious Cycle of Chronic Pain

Chronic pain creates a self-perpetuating feedback loop where pain, thoughts, feelings, and behaviours constantly influence each other, making the world feel smaller and smaller. The goal of CBT is to find a place to intervene and break this cycle.

Breaking the 'Boom & Bust' Cycle

A common trap is overdoing it on good days, leading to a crash. Pacing is the skill of using time, not pain, as a guide to slowly and steadily increase activity without causing a flare-up.

The 'Boom & Bust' Trap

Activity levels are erratic. Pushing too hard on a good day (boom) leads to a painful crash (bust), resulting in no consistent progress and increased frustration.

The Pacing Method

Activity is increased in small, planned, and consistent steps. This retrains the body and brain, building stamina and confidence while keeping pain stable.

A 3-Phase Plan for Progress

Recovery is a journey, not a single event. This structured plan breaks the process down into manageable phases, each building on the last to create lasting skills and confidence.

WEEKS 1-3

Phase 1: Laying the Groundwork

Establish a collaborative partnership. Start gathering data with an activity/mood log. Set tiny, achievable functional goals (SMART goals). Introduce the core skill of Pacing.

WEEKS 4-8

Phase 2: Behavioural Activation

Systematically re-engage with life. Create a 'Pleasant Activities Menu' and schedule them into the day. Leverage passions (like fashion/beauty) and create a gradual plan to return to school.

WEEKS 9-14

Phase 3: Cognitive Restructuring

Learn to identify, challenge, and reframe unhelpful 'Pain Thoughts'. Recognize common 'Thinking Traps' and develop a list of realistic, positive coping statements to use in difficult moments.

Pain vs. Suffering

Pain is a sensation. Suffering is the mental anguish we add on top. By detaching from the *outcome* of being pain-free, we can reduce suffering even when pain is present.

📦

Samskaras

Unprocessed emotional experiences can leave "imprints" (Samskaras) that manifest as physical pain. Healing involves acknowledging and 'digesting' these stored emotions.

🎯

Dharma & Dopamine

Her interests (beauty, fashion) are clues to her purpose (Dharma). Nurturing them builds an identity stronger than pain and helps reset the brain's reward (dopamine) system away from the phone trap.

Spotting Common Thinking Traps

Our minds have mental habits that can fuel the pain cycle. Helping your daughter notice and name these traps is the first step to changing them.

🔮

Catastrophizing

Assuming the worst possible outcome.
"This pain will never end. I'll be like this forever."

🌓

All-or-Nothing

Seeing things in extremes, with no middle ground.
"The day was a total failure because my pain flared up."

🧠

Mind Reading

Assuming you know what others are thinking negatively about you.
"They all think I'm just being lazy."
